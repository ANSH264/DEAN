About Prof. Nisanth N. Nair

Prof. Nisanth N. Nair is a Professor in the Department of Chemistry at IIT Kanpur, where he leads a research group focused on the cutting edge of Theoretical and Computational Chemistry. Since joining the institute in 2008, his work has significantly advanced the development of ab initio molecular dynamics and hybrid quantum mechanics/molecular mechanics (QM/MM) methodologies.

Academic Journey

Prof. Nair’s academic foundation is rooted in both Indian and international excellence. He earned his MSc in Chemistry from IIT Madras (2001) before moving to Germany to complete his PhD at Universität Hannover (2004). Following a postdoctoral research tenure at Ruhr-Universität Bochum, he returned to India to begin his faculty career at IIT Kanpur.

For more information visit here: https://home.iitk.ac.in/~nnair

Research Interests

His research explores the complex dynamics of chemical reactions across various environments, including:

Methodological Innovation

Developing new techniques for free energy calculations and accurate transferable all-atom force fields.

Catalysis & Enzymes

Studying reaction mechanisms in solution, solid catalysts (zeolites), and enzymatic systems.

Biochemical Impact

Investigating antibiotic resistance mechanisms related to beta-Lactamase.

Materials Science

Designing novel polyimides for advanced applications.

Research

Computational Chemistry

Our research focuses on the development of ab initio molecular dynamics techniques and their applications in studying chemical reactions in solution, enzymes, and various solid catalysts.

We work on the development of new methods for free energy calculations, novel hybrid quantum mechanics/molecular mechanics methods, and accurate transferable all-atom force fields.

Our interests also include understanding the mechanism of antibiotic resistance due to β-Lactamase, predicting mechanism and kinetics of catalytic reactions involving zeolites, and design of new polyimides.
